FAQ

Common Questions About JobQual

Everything you need to know about our outbound voice vetting, TCPA compliance, and scheduling systems.

How does the Outbound Call Screener work?

When a candidate submits their resume (or is imported into JobQual), the system calculates their initial semantic score. If they meet the target threshold, JobQual initiates an outbound call to qualify them. It verifies critical requirements (knockouts), asks role-specific technical questions, and scores their answers dynamically against your playbook rubric.

Is the outbound calling engine TCPA compliant?

Yes. JobQual integrates strict calling windows. It geocodes the candidate's phone number and ZIP code to determine their local time and only places calls between 8 AM and 9 PM candidate-local time. Additionally, we reconcile numbers against DNC block-lists before executing any outbound campaigns.

What is Blended Semantic Matching?

JobQual uses a multi-faceted matching algorithm to evaluate profiles: 70% is derived from a semantic context match of the whole resume against the JD, 25% looks at the "section-best" match to find hidden gems, and 5% acts as a geocoded location bonus based on Haversine distance. We also factor in career stability signals like average tenure and relocation history.

How does it integrate with our ATS?

We offer native integrations with Greenhouse and support custom webhooks. When a phone screen is completed, JobQual automatically pushes the structured rubric scorecard, the audio recording, and a complete text transcript directly into the candidate's ATS record.
